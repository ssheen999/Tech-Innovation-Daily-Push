### Paper of the Day: AI Can Learn Scientific Taste
**Source:** [arXiv 2603.14473](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14473)
**Authors:** Jingqi Tong, Mingzhe Li, Hangcheng Li, et al. (23 authors)
**Topic:** LLM · Scientific Reasoning · Reinforcement Learning

**What it proposes:**
Can an AI judge whether a research idea is worth pursuing? This paper says yes. The authors tackle a largely ignored problem: AI systems are good at *executing* research tasks, but terrible at the upstream judgment of *which* ideas have high potential. They train two models — a Scientific Judge and a Scientific Thinker — to fill this gap.

**Key insight:**
They introduce **RLCF (Reinforcement Learning from Community Feedback)** — training the judge on 700,000 citation-matched paper pairs, using citation counts as a proxy for scientific impact. The judge then serves as the reward model for the thinker, which learns to generate research proposals likely to have real-world impact. Crucially, the judge generalizes to *future* years and *unseen fields*, not just its training distribution.

**Why it matters for engineers:**
This is a template for building domain-specific taste models. Anywhere you have implicit human quality signals (upvotes, citations, stars, code reviews) you can train a judge. Applications: code quality judges, design critique agents, product idea screeners.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Read time:** ~25 min

### Technique Snapshot: RLCF — Reinforcement Learning from Community Feedback

**Concept:** RLCF (Reinforcement Learning from Community Feedback)

**One-line definition:** Train a reward model on large-scale community signals (citations, upvotes, stars) instead of expensive human preference labels, then use it to fine-tune a policy via RL.

**How it differs from RLHF:**
RLHF requires curated human comparisons — slow, expensive, bottlenecked by annotator bandwidth. RLCF substitutes crowd-aggregated signals that exist at massive scale in the wild. Trade-off: noisier signal, but orders of magnitude more data.

**Why it's relevant today:** As AI is applied to more specialized domains (law, science, engineering), gathering human preference labels at scale becomes impractical. RLCF offers a path to domain-specific quality signals without hiring armies of domain experts.

**Go deeper:** [arXiv 2603.14473](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14473) · [RLHF explainer by Lilian Weng](https://lilianweng.github.io/posts/2023-01-27-the-transformer-family-v2/)
